Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is recommended for patients with lower back pain when symptoms persist for more than six weeks despite conservative treatment.

Conservative treatment may include physical therapy, activity modification, and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medications.

MRI should be considered earlier when neurological deficits, severe weakness, bowel dysfunction, or bladder dysfunction are present.

Routine imaging is not recommended during the first six weeks of uncomplicated lower back pain.

MRI findings should be interpreted alongside clinical symptoms and physical examination findings.
